The Milky Way is a vast spiral galaxy that is home to our solar system, including Earth. It spans approximately 100,000 light-years in diameter and contains around 200 to 400 billion stars, along with countless nebulae, star clusters, and other cosmic phenomena. Its distinctive spiral structure includes a central bulge, surrounded by four main arms that spiral outward. The galaxy is part of a group of galaxies known as the Local Group, and it orbits alongside other galaxies in this group.
